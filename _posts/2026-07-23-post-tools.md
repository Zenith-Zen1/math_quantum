## Tools

### Mathematics: Number theory, modular arithmetic, linear algebra, probability, and algorithms
Conventional encryption systems like RSA use modular number theories to encode messages, making them virtually impossible to decrypt a non-quantum computer. 

### Quantum Computing: Qubits, quantum gates, quantum circuits, Shor’s algorithm, and Grover’s algorithm
Quantum bits, called qubits, are unlike normal bits. Normal bits can be either 0 or 1. Qubits can exist in a superposition of 0 and 1, holding two values at the same time until they are measured. Qubits can also be entangled, meaning that the value of one qubit depends on another, disregarding physical distance.
