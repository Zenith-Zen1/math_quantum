## Ethics
The intersection of quantum computing and cryptography raises important ethical questions about privacy, security, and fairness. A powerful quantum computer could potentially break current encryption systems, exposing private messages, financial records, medical information, and government data. This creates concerns about malicious use and “harvest now, decrypt later,” in which encrypted data is collected today and stored until quantum computers can decrypt it.

There are also questions about responsibility and access. Governments, researchers, and technology companies must decide when to disclose quantum-related security risks and how quickly to replace vulnerable systems. If only wealthy countries or corporations can access advanced quantum technology and quantum-safe security, existing inequalities could increase. Researchers should therefore develop quantum computing responsibly, protect sensitive information, and ensure that the transition to post-quantum cryptography is accessible, transparent, and fair.
